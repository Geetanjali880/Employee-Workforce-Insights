# **Employee Workforce Analysis Dashboard**

## **Project Description**
This project involves building an **interactive Power BI dashboard** to analyze a dataset containing key employee information. The dataset includes various attributes such as **age**, **business travel frequency**, **department**, **distance from home**, **education field**, **gender**, **job role**, **job satisfaction**, **marital status**, **monthly income**, **overtime status**, **total working years**, and **years at the company**. 

The goal of this analysis is to identify **trends and patterns** related to employee demographics, job satisfaction, income distribution, and work experience.

## **Dataset Overview**
The dataset consists of the following columns:

- **Index**: Unique identifier for each employee.
- **Age**: Age of the employee.
- **BusinessTravel**: Frequency of business travel (e.g., Rarely, Frequently).
- **Department**: The department the employee works in (e.g., Sales, Research & Development).
- **DistanceFromHome**: Distance from the employee's home to the workplace.
- **EducationField**: Employee’s field of education.
- **Gender**: Gender of the employee (Male/Female).
- **JobRole**: Employee’s job role in the company (e.g., Manager, Sales Executive).
- **JobSatisfaction**: Employee’s self-reported job satisfaction level (on a scale of 1 to 4).
- **MaritalStatus**: Marital status of the employee (Single, Married, Divorced).
- **MonthlyIncome**: Employee’s monthly salary.
- **OverTime**: Indicates if the employee works overtime (Yes/No).
- **TotalWorkingYears**: Total number of years the employee has been working.
- **YearsAtCompany**: Number of years the employee has been with the current company.

## **Objective**
The objective is to use **Power BI** to visualize the data, enabling a deeper understanding of the workforce's characteristics, and assisting in decision-making related to employee management, satisfaction, and retention strategies.

## **Data Cleaning**
The following key data cleaning steps were taken:

- **Handle missing values**: Filled missing values or removed rows with critical missing data.
- **Remove duplicates**: Identified and removed duplicate entries.
- **Correct data types**: Ensured proper data types (e.g., numeric for **Age** and **MonthlyIncome**, categorical for **BusinessTravel**).
- **Standardize categorical values**: Ensured consistency across categorical columns (e.g., **Gender**, **JobRole**).
